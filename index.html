10 Core Principles of Self-Sovereign Identity (Christopher Allen)

"The 10 Principles of Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI)" provide a foundational framework for Sovereing-T3 Protocol as well as understanding and implementing self-sovereign identity. These principles are designed to guide the development of systems that put users in control of their digital identities, ensuring privacy, security, and full personal autonomy. Here's a detailed explanation of each principle:

  1. Existence: Individuals must have an independent existence.
  2. Control: Users must control their identities.
  3. Access: Users must have access to their own data.
  4. Transparency: Systems and algorithms must be transparent.
  5. Persistence: Identities must be long-lived.
  6. Portability: Information and services about identity must be transportable.
  7. Interoperability: Identities should be as widely usable as possible.
  8. Consent: Users must consent to the use of their identity.
  9. Minimalization: Disclosure of claims must be minimized.
  10. Protection: The rights of users must be protected.

Why Sovereign-T3?

The term "sovereignty" traditionally refers to the supreme authority or power to govern oneself without interference from external sources. In political contexts, it often pertains to the autonomy and self-governance of a nation-state. In the context of blockchain technology, the concept of sovereignty takes on a new and powerful meaning, aligning with the core principles of autonomy, decentralization, trust, and transparency that blockchain aims to achieve. Blockchain technology, at its essence, seeks to provide a decentralized and trustless system where individuals have greater control over their data, assets, and interactions. Let's break down how the concept of sovereignty manifests in the core principles of blockchain:

  1. Autonomy: In a blockchain network, each participant has autonomy over their own actions and assets. Smart contracts, which operate based on predefined rules without the need for intermediaries, enhance this autonomy. Participants have control over their transactions and can engage in peer-to-peer interactions without relying on a central authority.
  2. Decentralization: Sovereignty is closely tied to the decentralization aspect of blockchain. In a decentralized network, there is no single point of control or failure. Participants collectively contribute to the governance and maintenance of the network, ensuring that decisions are made through consensus rather than by a central authority. This decentralization promotes sovereignty by distributing power across the network.
  3. Trust: Blockchain's distributed ledger and consensus mechanisms contribute to trust within the network. Participants can trust the system's integrity, as the transparent and immutable nature of the blockchain ensures that once a transaction is recorded, it cannot be altered. Sovereignty is reflected in the trust that participants place in the technology itself, rather than relying on trust in centralized entities.
  4. Transparency: Sovereignty and transparency go hand in hand. Blockchain provides a transparent and auditable record of transactions visible to all participants. This transparency enhances the trustworthiness of the system and empowers individuals with a clear view of the network's operations. Participants can independently verify transactions and ensure the integrity of the system.

Now, let's consider the term "Sovereign-T3" where 'T' stands for Trust, Transparency, and Total Autonomy:

  • Trust: Trust is fundamental to the concept of sovereignty within the context of blockchain. Sovereign-T3 emphasizes the establishment of trust through the inherent features of blockchain, enabling participants to transact and interact with confidence.
  • Transparency: Transparency is a key pillar of Sovereign-T3. The 'T' underscores the commitment to providing a clear and open view of the network's operations, ensuring that participants can verify transactions and governance processes independently.
  • Total Autonomy: The term "Total Autonomy" in Sovereign-T3 reinforces the idea that participants have complete control and self-governance over their digital identities, assets, and interactions within the blockchain network. It encapsulates the essence of sovereignty in the digital realm.

In summary, the concept of sovereignty is deeply ingrained in blockchain technology, and the term "Sovereign-T3" further emphasizes the commitment to decentralized trustless environment, transparency, and total autonomy within the blockchain ecosystem.

Introduction

In the evolving landscape of blockchain technology, Sovereign-T3 Protocol represents a paradigm shift by merging the capabilities of an Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM) compatible blockchain with the transformative power of Self-Sovereign Identity. The integration of SSI within the blockchain architecture empowers individuals with ownership and control over their digital identities, eliminating the need for centralized authorities to validate and authenticate personal information.

Key Components of Sovereign-T3 Protocol

  1. EVM Compatibility: Sovereign-T3 Protocol leverages the Ethereum Virtual Machine, allowing developers to seamlessly migrate existing Ethereum-based decentralized applications (DApps) to the Sovereign-T3 blockchain. This compatibility ensures a familiar and robust development environment for the blockchain community, promoting rapid adoption and innovation along with interoperability.
  2. Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI): At the core of Sovereign-T3 Protocol lies the implementation of Self-Sovereign Identity principles. Users are provided with cryptographic keys that serve as the foundation for creating, updating, and controlling their identity. This decentralized identity model ensures user privacy, security, and interoperability across various applications. Core benefits of SSI integrated into a blockchain architecture are:
    • Advanced Credential Verification - Elevate the reliability of credentials with flexible proof mechanisms, ensuring cryptographic validity and swift suspension or revocation when needed.
    • Decentralized Identity Establishment - Empower institutions and learners to assert identity autonomously, breaking free from reliance on centralized authorities.
    • Efficient Verifiable Data Registries - Revolutionize the status tracking of credentials with decentralized registries, eliminating the need for contacting issuing authorities.
    • Privacy-Centric Credential Status Checks - Safeguard individual privacy during credential verification, allowing thorough checks without compromising personal data.
    • Empowering Personal Data Management - Take control of your data with standards supporting individual authority over data sharing and access.
    • Selective Data Disclosure - Grant individuals the freedom to share specific portions of a larger dataset, putting them in control of their information.
    • User-Initiated Data Processing - Put individuals in control by allowing them to explicitly request any computation of their data, ensuring privacy and consent.
    • Progressive Information Sharing - Redefine information sharing with a progressive disclosure model, starting with the minimum required and gradually increasing disclosure.
    • Strategic Identity Proofing - Integrate personally identifiable information judiciously within credentials, enhancing utility within digital identity frameworks.
    • Minimal Data Collection Practices - Uphold privacy principles by collecting only the absolute minimum information required for any transaction.
  3. Decentralized Smart Contracts: Smart contracts are a fundamental aspect of Sovereign-T3 Protocol, facilitating automated and trustless execution of predefined rules. These contracts enable a wide array of applications, ranging from identity verification to complex financial transactions, to operate within the decentralized ecosystem securely. Here are key insights into how decentralized smart contracts aid in building such an architecture:
    • Immutable Identity Records - Smart contracts on the blockchain provide a tamper-proof and immutable ledger for recording identity-related transactions. SSI relies on the permanence and integrity of identity data, and smart contracts ensure that once information is recorded, it cannot be altered or deleted without consensus from the network.
    • Self-Executing Identity Transactions - Smart contracts enable self-executing identity transactions, automating various aspects of identity management. For instance, a smart contract can automatically update a user's credentials when they achieve a milestone or complete a degree or acquire a technical qualification. This automation reduces the reliance on manual processes and enhances the efficiency of identity updates.
    • Selective Disclosure and Privacy-Preserving Transactions - Smart contracts allow for the implementation of selective disclosure mechanisms. Users can execute smart contracts to selectively disclose specific identity attributes or credentials, maintaining control over the information they share without revealing unnecessary details. This supports the privacy-centric nature of SSI.
    • Decentralized Governance and Revocation - Smart contracts enable decentralized governance models for identity management. The community or network participants can collectively decide on the rules and policies governing identity issuance, updates, and revocation. If the need arises to suspend or revoke a credential, the decision-making process can be executed through smart contracts, promoting transparency and fairness.
    • Interoperability and Standardization - Smart contracts can be programmed to adhere to identity-related standards and protocols, fostering interoperability between different SSI-enabled systems. This ensures that decentralized identities and credentials issued on one blockchain can be recognized and accepted by others, creating a more connected and versatile identity ecosystem.
    • Event-Driven Identity Updates - Decentralized smart contracts can be triggered by specific events, such as a change in employment status or completion of a certification. This event-driven model ensures that identity updates occur in response to real-world changes, keeping the identity information current and accurate.
    • Decentralized & Trustless Credential Verification - The trustless and decentralized nature of smart contracts ensures that credential verification can occur without the need for intermediaries or trusted third parties. This aligns with the core principle of trustlessness in blockchain technology and enhances the security and reliability of the identity verification process. Instead of relying on a central authority for verification, smart contracts can enable a consensus-based approach where multiple parties in the network validate the presented credentials, enhancing the overall trustworthiness of the identity.

Technical Architecture

  1. Consensus Mechanism: Sovereign-T3 Protocol employs a robust consensus mechanism, ensuring the integrity and security of the blockchain. The consensus mechanism may be based on Proof-of-Stake (PoS), Proof-of-Work (PoW), or a hybrid model, providing flexibility based on the specific requirements and goals of the blockchain network.
  2. Interoperability: The blockchain is designed to promote interoperability with other blockchains, ensuring seamless communication and data exchange. Interoperability allows users to leverage their SSI across multiple platforms, enhancing the overall utility of their decentralized identities.
  3. Data Encryption and Privacy: To uphold the principles of SSI, Sovereign-T3 Protocol incorporates advanced cryptographic techniques to encrypt and protect user data. This emphasis on privacy ensures that personal information remains confidential, and users have granular control over what information is shared and with whom.
  4. Scalability: Recognizing the importance of scalability in a blockchain network, Sovereign-T3 Protocol employs innovative solutions such as sharding or layer 2 scaling solutions. This ensures that the network can handle a growing number of users and transactions without compromising performance.

The leading framework under consideration to build Sovereign-T3 Protocol is Cosmos SDK as it allows building application specific blockchains. Application-specific blockchains are emerging as a powerful tool in the world of blockchain technology, offering several significant benefits across various dimensions such as flexibility, performance, security, and sovereignty. These blockchains are distinct in their approach, allowing developers to tailor their blockchain network specifically for a particular application, which stands in contrast to the more general-purpose nature of traditional blockchains.

  • Flexibility: One of the key advantages of application-specific blockchains is the high degree of flexibility they offer to developers. In the Cosmos ecosystem, for example, the state-machine of a blockchain is linked to the consensus engine via the Application Blockchain Interface (ABCI). This interface is language-agnostic, meaning developers can build their state-machine in any programming language. This freedom extends to the choice of development frameworks as well, with options like Cosmos SDK, Lotion, and Weave available, each supporting different programming languages. Moreover, the ABCI allows for the substitution of the consensus engine. Currently, CometBFT is the primary option, but there's potential for more in the future. Developers also have the liberty to fine-tune the chosen framework and consensus engine to better suit their specific needs. They can make decisions on a range of trade-offs, such as the number of validators versus transaction throughput or safety versus availability, and on design choices like database types or account models. The Cosmos SDK even permits automatic execution of code at specific times, and developers can choose their cryptographic library, offering a level of customization not typically available in virtual-machine blockchains.
  • Performance: In terms of performance, application-specific blockchains have a distinct edge over decentralized applications built on smart contracts. Since these blockchains are dedicated to a single application, they avoid the competition for computational and storage resources seen in non-sharded virtual-machine blockchains. The use of efficient consensus mechanisms like CometBFT, as opposed to the more commonly used Proof-of-Work in virtual-machine blockchains, significantly boosts throughput. The primary bottleneck in transaction processing is the state-machine, and by avoiding the need for transactions to be processed by a virtual machine, application-specific blockchains can greatly reduce computational complexity.
  • Security: Security considerations are also a vital aspect of application-specific blockchains. Developers can leverage established programming languages like Go, avoiding the pitfalls of immature smart contract languages. They are not restricted to the cryptographic functions of underlying virtual machines and can employ custom cryptography with well-audited libraries. Furthermore, the absence of an underlying virtual machine reduces the risk of bugs or exploitable mechanisms, simplifying the security analysis of the application.
  • Sovereignty: Perhaps the most significant advantage of application-specific blockchains is the sovereignty they provide. In a decentralized application ecosystem, various actors are involved, including users, developers, and third-party services. When applications are built on shared virtual-machine blockchains, the community of the application is often overshadowed by the community governing the underlying blockchain. This misalignment can lead to challenges in updating or fixing the application if the broader community is unresponsive. Application-specific blockchains solve this governance misalignment by granting full control to the stakeholders of the application over the entire chain. This ensures that the community is not immobilized by discovered bugs and has the freedom to evolve as it sees fit. This level of sovereignty is crucial for the long-term viability and adaptability of decentralized applications.

Applications of Sovereign-T3 Protocol

  1. Identity Verification: The Sovereign-T3 Protocol serves as a foundation for secure and efficient identity verification processes. From birth and death registration to academic and professional journey tracking, individuals can maintain a comprehensive and tamper-proof record of their life events, accessible only with their explicit consent.
  2. Membership Access and Credentials: Organizations and membership-based entities can utilize Sovereign-T3 Protocol to manage access control and issue verifiable credentials. This can range from granting access to exclusive content to providing proof of membership qualifications, all while maintaining the privacy and security of the individual's identity.
  3. Decentralized Finance (DeFi): Sovereign-T3 Protocol opens the door for decentralized financial applications, enabling users to participate in a variety of financial services such as lending, borrowing, and trading. The secure and verifiable identity provided by SSI enhances the overall trust and transparency in DeFi applications.
  4. Supply Chain Management: The blockchain's transparency and traceability features make it well-suited for supply chain management. Sovereign-T3 Protocol can be utilized to track and authenticate the origin and journey of products, ensuring accountability and mitigating the risks of fraud within the supply chain.

Major Challenges

  1. Regulatory Compliance: The implementation of SSI and its application in various sectors may face regulatory challenges. Sovereign-T3 Protocol aims to work collaboratively with regulators to ensure compliance with legal frameworks while preserving the principles of decentralized identity.
  2. User Adoption and Education: The success of Sovereign-T3 Protocol relies on widespread user adoption and understanding of the benefits of self-sovereign identity. Educational initiatives and user-friendly interfaces are essential to bridge the knowledge gap and encourage individuals and organizations to embrace the new paradigm.
